# Evergreen Huckleberry - *Vaccinium ovatum*

A versatile Pacific Northwest native evergreen shrub prized for its intense, sweet-tart berries and year-round ornamental value.

## Growth Habit
*   **Full Sun:** Stays compact (3–5 ft) and bushy. Produces the highest fruit yields.
*   **Partial/Full Shade:** Grows taller and more graceful (up to 12 ft), but fruit production is significantly lower.
*   **Foliage:** Small, glossy, leathery evergreen leaves. New growth is often bronze or bright red.

## Care (Zone 8b/9a)
*   **Soil:** Must have **acidic, well-drained soil** (pH 4.5–6.0). Thrives with high organic matter.
*   **Water:** Once established, it is remarkably drought-tolerant. Provide deep water in July/August to ensure juicy berries.
*   **Maintenance:** Minimal. Slow-growing but handles pruning or shearing well if used as a hedge.
*   **Fertilizer:** Use light applications of acid-loving plant food in early spring.

## Edibility & Harvest
*   **Flavor:** Intense, wild berry flavor—sweeter and more complex than commercial blueberries.
*   **Harvest Season:** Late August through November or December.
*   **Sweetness Secret:** Wait until **after the first light frost** to harvest; the cold converts starches to sugars, making the fruit much sweeter.
*   **Usage:** Excellent fresh, in baked goods, or made into jams/jellies.

## Propagation
*   **Seeds:** Most reliable for large quantities; requires 1–3 months of cold, moist stratification. Sow on the surface of an acidic mix.
*   **Cuttings:** Difficult; take semi-hardwood cuttings in late April with rooting hormone and provide basal heat if possible.
*   **Layering:** Slow but effective; pin a branch to the ground for a full year until a sufficient root system develops.

## Wildlife
*   Attracts bees and hummingbirds during spring flowering.
*   Provides essential winter forage for birds.
