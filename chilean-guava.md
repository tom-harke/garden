# Chilean Guava - *Ugni molinae*

Also known as **Strawberry Myrtle** or **Ugni**, this is a slow-growing evergreen shrub prized for its uniquely delicious fruit and ornamental foliage.

## Care in Zone 8b/9a
*   **Hardiness:** Hardy to ~15°F. Protect with mulch or frost cloth during extreme "Polar Vortex" events.
*   **Light:** **Full sun to partial shade.** In the PNW, it thrives in most light conditions but appreciates some afternoon shade during heatwaves.
*   **Soil:** Prefers **well-drained, acidic soil** (pH 5.5–6.5). Avoid waterlogged winter soil.
*   **Water:** Keep consistently moist, especially during fruit development. Moderately drought-tolerant once established.
*   **Habit:** Slow-growing evergreen. Takes well to pruning and can be grown as a hedge or in containers.

## Propagation
* **Cuttings:** Highly successful; 4-6" semi-ripe cuttings in late summer or autumn.
* **Layering:** Stems can be layered in autumn for new plants the following year.
* **Seeds:** Possible but slow; sow in spring after cleaning seeds.

## Edibility & Harvest
*   **Flavor Profile:** A complex blend of **wild strawberry, pineapple, and bubblegum**.
*   **Harvesting:** Ripens in late summer to autumn (Sept–Oct). Berries turn a deep burgundy-red when ripe.
*   **Uses:** Excellent fresh, in jams, or as a flavorful addition to desserts.

## Ornamental Value
*   Glossy, small evergreen leaves with a spicy scent when crushed.
*   Produce fragrant, nodding, bell-shaped flowers in late spring/early summer.
