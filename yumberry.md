# Yumberry (Yangmei) - *Myrica rubra*

Also known as **Red Bayberry**.

It is a prized evergreen fruit tree native to East Asia.


## Characteristics
*   **Fruit:** Round, bumpy-textured fruit (1.5–2.5 cm). Deep red to purple-black when ripe. 
*   **Flavor:** Sweet-tart; profile similar to a blend of pomegranate, strawberry, and raspberry.
*   **Growth Habit:** Evergreen tree or large shrub, reaching 15–30 feet if left unpruned, but can be maintained smaller.
*   **Nitrogen Fixer:** Like other *Myrica* species, it improves soil quality by fixing nitrogen.
*   **Evergreen**

## Growing Conditions (Zone 8b/9a)
*   **Hardiness:** Marginally hardy (Zones 8–10). Mature trees handle 15°F, but young plants need protection.
*   **Drainage:** **Critical.** Highly susceptible to root rot in waterlogged winter soil. Plant on a mound or in raised beds.
*   **Soil:** Prefers **acidic soil (pH 4.5–5.5)**. Similar requirements to Blueberries or Honeyberries.
*   **Light:** Full sun. Needs high summer heat units to ripen fruit and maximize sweetness.
*   **Pollination:** Usually **dioecious** (separate male and female plants). At least one of each is required for fruit. Some rare cultivars claim partial self-fertility.

## Propagation
*   **Seeds:** Requires 6–8 weeks of cold stratification to break dormancy; fresh seeds from ripe fruit have the highest success rate.
*   **Cuttings:** Take semi-hardwood cuttings in late summer; use rooting hormone and maintain high humidity.
*   **Grafting:** Frequently used to propagate high-quality cultivars onto hardy rootstocks.

## Cultivars to Consider
*   **Biqi:** Deep purple, smaller fruit, ripens early. Best candidate for the PNW's shorter heat window.
*   **Purple Pearl:** Large, sweet, high-quality fruit.
*   **Eastern Giant:** Known for its size.

## Maintenance & Care
*   **Winter Protection:** Protect young trees from hard freezes with frost cloth or move containers to a sheltered area.
*   **Mulching:** Use acidic mulch (pine needles, wood chips) to protect shallow roots and maintain moisture.
*   **Microclimate:** In the PNW, plant against a south-facing wall to trap heat.
