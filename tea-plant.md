# Tea Plant - *Camellia sinensis*

The true tea plant is an evergreen shrub in the Camellia family. All types of tea—white, green, oolong, and black—are produced from the leaves of this single species.

## Growth Habit
*   **Size:** Can grow to 6–10 feet, but is often pruned to a "plucking table" height of 3–4 feet for easier harvesting.
*   **Foliage:** Glossy, dark green, serrated evergreen leaves.
*   **Flowers:** Small, fragrant, nodding white flowers with yellow stamens in late fall/early winter.

## Care in Zone 8b (Portland)
*   **Hardiness:** Reliably hardy in Zone 8b. Young plants should be protected from "Arctic blasts" (below 20°F) with mulch and frost cloth.
*   **Light:** **Morning sun with afternoon shade** is ideal. Protect from intense, direct late-afternoon summer sun to prevent leaf scorch.
*   **Soil:** Requires **acidic soil** (pH 5.5–6.5) and excellent drainage. Portland’s naturally acidic soil is well-suited, but heavy clay should be amended with organic matter.
*   **Water:** Needs consistent moisture, especially during the dry summer months. Do not allow the soil to dry out completely.
*   **Fertilizer:** Use an acid-loving fertilizer (like those for Azaleas/Rhododendrons) in early spring.

## Propagation
*   **Cuttings:** Semi-hardwood cuttings taken in late summer or autumn are the standard method for cloning high-quality plants.
*   **Seeds:** Possible but slow; soak seeds for 24–48 hours before planting and expect 1–3 months for germination.
*   **Layering:** Air layering works well for mature shrubs and is a reliable way to produce large new plants.

## Edibility & Harvest
*   **Harvesting:** The highest quality tea is made from the "fine pluck"—the **top two leaves and the unopened leaf bud** at the tip of each branch.
*   **Timing:** In the PNW, the "first flush" usually starts in late April or May.
*   **Processing:**
    *   **Green Tea:** Steam or pan-fry immediately after picking to prevent oxidation.
    *   **Black Tea:** Wither and bruise the leaves, allow to fully oxidize (turn brown), then dry.
*   **Flowers:** The flowers are also edible and can be used as a garnish or dried for tea blends.

## Garden Role
*   **Edible Hedge:** Responds well to pruning and can be grown as a dense, evergreen privacy screen.
*   **Ornamental Shrub:** Provides year-round structure and late-season flowers when many other plants are dormant.
