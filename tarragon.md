# Tarragon - *Artemisia dracunculus*

A prized culinary herb known for its distinct anise/licorice flavor.

## Variety Focus: **French Tarragon**
*   **Flavor:** Sweet, intense anise aroma.
*   **Warning:** Buy started plants only. Seeds are usually "Russian" tarragon, which is vigorous but has a bitter, grassy flavor.

## Care (Zone 8b/9a)
*   **Light:** Full sun to partial shade. In the PNW, full sun helps prevent mildew.
*   **Soil:** Rich but **exceptionally well-drained**. Prone to root rot in winter.
*   **Water:** Likes consistent but light moisture. Do not overwater.
*   **Winter:** Completely **herbaceous**. It will disappear underground in late autumn. Mark the spot so you don't dig it up!
*   **Maintenance:** Divide the root clump every 3 years in spring to maintain flavor and vigor.

## Propagation
*   **Division:** French tarragon is sterile and must be propagated by root division in early spring as it emerges.
*   **Cuttings:** Take 4–6 inch stem cuttings in late spring or early summer; root them in a well-draining medium under high humidity.
*   **Note:** Do not grow French tarragon from seed; seeds sold as tarragon are usually the inferior Russian variety.

## Culinary Uses
*   Essential for French cuisine (Béarnaise sauce).
*   Pairs perfectly with chicken, fish, eggs, and vinegar.
