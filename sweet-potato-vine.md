# Sweet Potato Vine - *Ipomoea batatas*

A vigorous, heat-loving plant grown for its edible tubers and greens, or for its striking ornamental foliage.

## Types
*   **Ornamental:** Prized for foliage color (purple, chartreuse, variegated). Edible but poor-quality tubers.
*   **Edible:** Bred specifically for large, sweet tubers. Aggressive spreading habit.
*   **Dual-Purpose (Treasure Island™ Series):** Cultivars bred for both ornamental beauty and delicious edible tubers.

## Care (Zone 8b/9a)
*   **Sun:** **Full Sun.** Required for tuber development. Ornamental varieties need sun for vibrant color but can tolerate partial shade.
*   **Water:** **High.** Needs consistent moisture (1" per week). Will wilt quickly in PNW summer dry spells.
*   **Soil:** Loose, well-drained, sandy loam. In heavy PNW clay, use raised beds or large containers to help the soil warm up.
*   **Timing:** **Do not plant until June.** Soil must be at least 65°F. Early planting in cool PNW soil will stunt the plant.

## Edibility & Harvest
*   **Leaves/Greens:** All varieties have edible leaves. High in Vitamin A and C; eat raw or sautéed.
*   **Tubers:** Harvest edible varieties after the first frost kills the vines.
*   **Overwintering:** Dig up tubers in late Oct and store in a cool, dry place. Replant in June. Cuttings can be rooted in water and kept as houseplants.
