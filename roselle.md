# Roselle - *Hibiscus sabdariffa*

Also known as **Florida Cranberry** or **Red Sorrel**, this tropical shrub is famous for its fleshy red calyces used to make tart Hibiscus tea (*Agua de Jamaica*).

## Growth Habit
*   **Size:** Grows quickly to **4–6 feet tall** and 3 feet wide.
*   **Form:** Upright, bushy shrub with red stems and lobed green leaves.
*   **Flowers:** Creamy yellow flowers with a dark red center. The prize is the **calyx** (the fleshy base of the flower) that turns bright red and plump after the petals fall.

## Care in Zone 8b (Portland)
*   **Life Cycle:** In Portland, Roselle must be grown as a **warm-season annual**. It is killed by the first frost.
*   **Season Extension:** **Must be started indoors** 6–8 weeks before the last frost. It needs heat to grow; only move outdoors when night temperatures are consistently above 50°F (usually June).
*   **Light:** **Full Sun** is critical. Place it in the warmest part of your garden.
*   **Soil:** Rich, well-draining soil. It is a heavy feeder; add compost at planting.
*   **Water:** Needs consistent moisture during the summer heat.

## Propagation
*   **Seeds:** The most common method for annual growing. Soak seeds for 24 hours before sowing to speed up germination.
*   **Cuttings:** Possible in late summer, but since the plant is grown as an annual in the PNW, it is rarely done unless overwintering in a heated greenhouse.

## Edibility & Harvest
*   **Calyces:** Harvest about 10–14 days after the flowers fall. They should be firm and deep red. Used for teas, jams, and syrups.
*   **Leaves:** Young leaves are edible and have a tangy, citrusy flavor (similar to Sorrel).
*   **Flavor:** Intensely tart, similar to cranberry or rhubarb.

## Garden Role
*   **Edible Ornamental:** Its red stems and yellow flowers make it a beautiful addition to a sunny border.
*   **Summer Privacy Screen:** Grows fast enough to provide a temporary screen during the summer months.
