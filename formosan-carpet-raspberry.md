# Formosan Carpet Raspberry - *Rubus pentalobus*

Also known as **Creeping Raspberry** or **Crinkle-leaf Creeper**. Often sold as the cultivar **'Emerald Carpet'**.

An exceptionally tough, evergreen groundcover native to the mountains of Taiwan. It is highly valued in the Pacific Northwest for its ability to suppress weeds and stabilize soil while providing a modest edible harvest.

## Growth Habit
*   **Size:** Grows only **3–6 inches tall**.
*   **Spread:** Spreads vigorously via creeping runners that root at the nodes. A single plant can spread **3–5 feet** within a few seasons.
*   **Foliage:** Leathery, crinkled, heart-shaped leaves. Dark green in summer, turning a beautiful **burgundy or plum-purple** in winter.

## Care in Zone 8b (Portland)
*   **Sun:** Full sun to partial shade. Tighter growth and better fruiting occur in full sun.
*   **Soil:** Very adaptable; tolerates clay, sand, and poor soils. Requires decent drainage; avoid areas with standing winter water.
*   **Water:** Moderate water to establish. Once established (after 1 year), it is significantly **drought-tolerant**.
*   **Maintenance:** Low maintenance. Can be edged with shears if it encroaches on paths. In early spring, it can be mowed on a high setting to refresh old foliage if it becomes woody.

## Edibility & Wildlife
*   **Fruit:** Small, aggregate berries that turn **amber to salmon-orange** when ripe (mid-to-late summer).
*   **Flavor:** Mildly sweet and tart; similar to a raspberry but less intense.
*   **Wildlife:** Flowers are attractive to bees; dense mat provides habitat for beneficial insects.

## Propagation
*   **Layering:** Extremely easy; stems root wherever they touch the soil. Peg runners down to the soil to speed up the process.
*   **Division:** Dig up established rooted "plugs" or sections of the mat in spring or fall and transplant immediately.
*   **Cuttings:** Take 4–6 inch semi-hardwood cuttings in summer and keep in a humid environment until rooted.

## Garden Role
*   **Edible Groundcover:** Best used as a "living mulch" under larger shrubs (like Blueberries or Figs).
*   **Erosion Control:** Excellent for stabilizing sunny or partially shaded banks.
*   **Paving Filler:** Can handle light foot traffic.
