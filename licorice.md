# Licorice - *Glycyrrhiza glabra*

A deciduous perennial legume grown for its sweet, medicinal roots.

## Care in Zone 8b/9a
*   **Soil:** Needs **deep, sandy/loamy soil** (at least 2 ft deep) for root expansion.
*   **Sun:** Full sun.
*   **Hardiness:** Root-hardy in Zone 8b; foliage dies back in winter.
*   **Management:** Can be aggressive once established; consider root barriers.

## Propagation
*   **Root Cuttings:** Take 2–4 inch sections of horizontal rhizomes in late winter or early spring, ensuring each has at least one bud.
*   **Seeds:** Soak seeds in warm water for 24 hours before sowing in spring to improve germination rates.

## Harvesting
*   **Patience:** Do not harvest until the plant is **3-4 years old**.
*   **Timing:** Harvest lateral roots in late autumn when the plant is dormant.
*   **Processing:** Wash and dry roots for storage. Used for teas, candy, and throat soothing.
