# Spineless Caper - *Capparis spinosa var. inermis*

A heat-loving, drought-tolerant Mediterranean shrub grown for its edible flower buds.

## Care in Zone 8b/9a
*   **Hardiness:** Root-hardy to ~18°F. The plant will likely die back to the ground in a freeze and regrow in spring.
*   **Sun:** **Full sun.** Place in the hottest, brightest spot available.
*   **Soil & Drainage:** **Exceptional drainage is mandatory.** Use a lean, mineral-rich soil mix (e.g., 50% potting soil, 25% sand, 25% perlite). Prone to root rot in wet soil.
*   **Container Growing:** Highly recommended for Zone 8b. Use terracotta pots for breathability and move to a sheltered spot (like a garage) during deep freezes.
*   **Pruning:** Prune heavily in late winter. Flowers form on **new wood**.

## Propagation
* **Cuttings:** Difficult; hardwood in late winter or semi-hardwood in summer with IBA and bottom heat.
* **Seeds:** Fresh seeds preferred; require 2 months cold stratification.
* **Layering:** Air layering or tip layering can be successful in summer.

## Harvesting
*   **Capers:** Harvest the immature flower buds while they are small and tight.
*   **Caperberries:** If buds are left to flower and fruit, the resulting "caperberries" are also edible (usually pickled).
*   **Flowers:** Beautiful white-and-purple blooms if not harvested for capers.
