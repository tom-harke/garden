# Wild Bergamot - *Monarda fistulosa*

A hardy, drought-tolerant native perennial that is more resistant to mildew than its red cousins.

## Care (Zone 8b/9a)
*   **Light:** Full sun to partial shade. Afternoon shade is beneficial in Zone 8b.
*   **Airflow:** **Critical.** Space plants 18-24" apart to prevent powdery mildew in humid PNW/Southern summers.
*   **Water:** Drought-tolerant once established, unlike *Monarda didyma*.

## Propagation
*   **Division:** Easiest method; divide root clumps in early spring as new growth appears.
*   **Seeds:** Sow in spring or autumn on the soil surface; they are tiny and may require light to germinate.
*   **Cuttings:** Take softwood cuttings in late spring and root them in a moist, well-draining medium.

## Benefits
*   **Wildlife:** Top-tier for bees, butterflies, and hummingbird moths.
*   **Culinary:** Spicy, savory leaves (oregano-like) for "Oswego Tea" or seasoning.
*   **Medicinal:** Antiseptic and soothing for digestive issues.
