# Huckleberries - *Vaccinium spp.*

Pacific Northwest native shrubs that produce some of the most flavorful wild berries in the world. They are perfectly adapted to the acidic, shady "Forest" zone.

## Varieties for Zone 8b
*   **Evergreen Huckleberry (*Vaccinium ovatum*):**
    *   **Habit:** Glossy, leathery evergreen leaves. Can be a 3-ft sun hedge or a 10-ft shade specimen.
    *   **Harvest:** Small, dark blue-black berries that ripen late (Oct–Nov). Best flavor after the first frost.
*   **Red Huckleberry (*Vaccinium parvifolium*):**
    *   **Habit:** Deciduous with bright green, angular stems. A "nurse log" specialist.
    *   **Harvest:** Translucent red, tart berries in mid-summer (July).
*   **Mountain Huckleberry (*Vaccinium membranaceum*):**
    *   **Habit:** Deciduous shrub with thin leaves that turn deep red in autumn.
    *   **Harvest:** The premier wild huckleberry. Large, sweet-tart purple berries in August.

## Care & Management
*   **Soil (Critical):** **Must be acidic (pH 4.5–5.5).** Use pine needles, bark, or sulfur to maintain acidity.
*   **Drainage:** They hate "wet feet." In Portland clay, plant on a slight mound or in a raised woodland bed.
*   **Moisture:** Requires consistent moisture, especially during fruit set. Mulching with wood chips or conifer needles is essential to keep roots cool.
*   **Fertilizer:** Very light feeders. Use an organic fertilizer designed for acid-loving plants (like Holly-tone) in early spring.

## Garden Role
*   **Forest Zone:** All huckleberries thrive here.
*   **Wildlife:** Exceptional for native bees (early spring flowers) and birds (berries).
*   **Aesthetic:** Provides multi-season interest from delicate spring blooms to vibrant fall color (for deciduous types).
