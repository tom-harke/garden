# Azalea - *Rhododendron*

Classic ornamental shrubs for the PNW, available in evergreen and deciduous varieties.

## Varieties for Zone 8b/9a
*   **Evergreen Azaleas:** Compact and dense. They retain leaves year-round and prefer morning sun with afternoon shade.
*   **Deciduous Azaleas:** Often highly fragrant with larger blooms. They lose their leaves in fall and are much more sun-tolerant than evergreens.

## Care (Zone 8b/9a)
*   **Soil:** Requires **acidic, well-drained soil (pH 4.5–6.0)**. Highly sensitive to "wet feet"; ensure excellent drainage.
*   **Sunlight:**
    *   *Evergreens:* Dappled or filtered shade. Intense afternoon sun can burn leaves.
    *   *Deciduous:* Can handle full sun (6+ hours).
*   **Planting Depth:** **Critical.** Set the root ball 1–2 inches above the soil grade. Planting too deep is a common cause of death.
*   **Mulch:** Apply 2–3 inches of acidic mulch (pine needles or bark) to protect shallow roots.

## Propagation
*   **Stem Cuttings:** Best for evergreen varieties. Take 3–5 inch semi-hardwood cuttings in late spring or early summer, use rooting hormone, and keep in high humidity; roots form in 4–8 weeks.
*   **Layering:** Highly successful for deciduous types. Pin a flexible, wounded branch to the soil; it typically takes 1–2 years to develop a strong root system.
*   **Seeds:** Used for species or new hybrids. Surface-sow on moist peat in late winter; do not cover as they need light to germinate.

## Maintenance
*   **Pruning:** Prune evergreen types immediately after they finish blooming. Deciduous types require minimal pruning.
*   **Pests:** Watch for Azalea Lace Bug, especially on evergreen varieties in too much sun.
