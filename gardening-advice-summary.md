# Gardening Summary & Strategy: Portland Metro

## Core Challenges
- **Drainage & Winter Wetness:** The primary threat to root systems in Portland's heavy soils.
- **Soil pH:** Crucial for acid-loving plants like blueberries and lingonberries.
- **Microclimate:** A great growing season, but autumn moisture requires disease-resistant, "closed-eye" varieties to prevent rot.

## Key Troubleshooting
- **Lingonberries, Currants, & Gooseberries:** Focus on drainage. If growing in native clay, transition to raised beds with a high-porosity mix (compost/potting soil/sand).
- **Blueberries:** Test pH (aim for 4.5–5.5). Amend with elemental sulfur if necessary. Ensure excellent drainage.
- **Figs:** Prioritize "closed-eye" cultivars to prevent fruit rot during autumn rains.
- **Citrus:** Expect challenges due to winter wetness. Provide maximum sun (south-facing wall) and winter protection.
- **Kiwi:** Setup identified (*Meader* male; *Anna, Cordifola, Ken's Red* females). **Focus on sturdy structural support** to handle heavy, wet foliage and fruit load.
- **Honeyberries:** With a robust collection (*Solo, Tundra, Indigo Gem, Beast, Berry Blue*), ensure adequate spacing and prune for airflow to prevent mildew.
- **Elderberry/Grapes/Boysenberry:** Keep monitor on the elderberry rooting. Ensure proper trellising for grapes and the boysenberry, which is a trailing, vigorous hybrid that thrives in the PNW.

## Common Disease: Powdery Mildew
In the PNW, "powdery white" leaves followed by leaf drop is a classic sign of **Powdery Mildew**. It thrives in humid air and fluctuating temps, regardless of sunlight levels.

### **Organic Control Recipes**
1. **Milk Spray (Antiseptic):** 
   - *Recipe:* 1 part milk (skim is best) to 3 parts water.
   - *Usage:* Spray in the **morning on a sunny day**. The proteins react with UV light to "burn" the fungus. Apply every 7-10 days.
2. **Baking Soda Spray (pH Disruptor):**
   - *Recipe:* 1 tbsp baking soda + 1 tsp liquid soap + 1 gallon water.
   - *Usage:* Changes leaf pH to prevent spores from germinating. Best as a preventative. **Do not apply in high heat (80°F+)** as it can burn leaves.

### **Cultural Prevention**
- **Airflow:** Prune the center of the plant to ensure wind can move through the foliage.
- **Water at the Base:** Keep foliage dry. Dew is enough to trigger mildew in the PNW; overhead watering makes it worse.
- **Cleanup:** Do not compost mildewed leaves; the spores can overwinter in the pile.

## 💡 Strategic Insights
- **The "Semicircle" Succession:** Planting **Strawberries over Tulips & Daffodils** is a masterstroke. The strawberries hide dying bulb foliage and act as a living mulch.
- **The "Brick Wall" Synergy:** This is your most valuable real estate for heat-lovers. Use the radiant heat to ripen **Citrus** and **Pomegranates**, while providing the "baked" soil needed for **Capers** and **Hens & Chicks**.
- **Mint Containment:** **Mint must remain in pots.** Due to its highly aggressive spreading habit, it should never be planted directly in the ground to avoid it overwhelming other garden zones. Best kept in the "Nursery" (Deck 4) for optimal health without the risk. *(Exception: [Corsican Mint](corsican-mint.md) is permitted as a groundcover in the Semicircle due to its diminutive size and manageable growth.)*

## Suggested Future Additions
- **Thornless Blackberries:** (*'Triple Crown'* or *'Columbia Star'*). Highly productive; requires trellising.
- **Alpine Strawberries:** (*'Alexandria'* or *'Yellow Wonder'*). Shade-tolerant; excellent ground cover.
- **Elderberries:** (*'Adams'* or *'Johns'*). Prolific; cross-pollination required.
- **Goumi:** Nitrogen-fixing; disease-resistant.
