# Bearberry - *Arctostaphylos uva-ursi*

Also known as **Kinnikinnick**, this is a versatile native PNW evergreen groundcover valued for its drought tolerance and year-round interest.

## Care in Zone 8b/9a
*   **Hardiness:** Exceptionally hardy (Zone 2-8).
*   **Light:** **Full sun** to partial shade. Best flowering and berry production occurs in full sun.
*   **Soil:** Must have **well-drained, acidic soil** (pH 4.5–5.5). Thrives in sandy, rocky, or poor soils. Avoid heavy clay.
*   **Water:** Highly drought-tolerant once established. Water regularly for the first 1-2 years.
*   **Transplanting:** Difficult to move once established due to a sensitive root system.

## Propagation
* **Cuttings:** Difficult; 3-6" softwood/semi-hardwood in summer with hormone.
* **Layering:** Naturally occurs as stems touch the ground; can be encouraged by pinning down branches.
* **Seeds:** Possible but slow; requires several months of cold stratification.

## Edibility & Uses
*   **Berries:** Bright red berries are bland/mealy raw but palatable when cooked into jams or jellies (tasting somewhat like tart cranberries).
*   **Medicinal:** Leaves are used for **Uva Ursi tea**, a traditional remedy for urinary tract health.
*   **Caution:** Consult a professional before medicinal use. Avoid during pregnancy.

## Landscape Use
*   Ideal for rock gardens, stabilizing slopes (erosion control), or cascading over walls.
*   Attracts bees and butterflies; berries provide winter food for birds.
