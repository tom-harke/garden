# Wintergreen - *Gaultheria procumbens*

A low-growing, aromatic, evergreen groundcover perfect for acid-loving shade gardens.

## Care in Zone 8b/9a
*   **Light:** Dappled shade to partial shade. Avoid direct afternoon sun.
*   **Soil:** **Must be acidic (pH < 6.0)** and rich in organic matter. Thrives in similar conditions as blueberries.
*   **Water:** Requires **consistent moisture**. Do not let dry out.
*   **Habit:** Spreads by underground rhizomes.

## Propagation
*   **Division:** The fastest method; dig up and divide rooted "runners" or clumps in early spring or autumn.
*   **Cuttings:** Take semi-ripe cuttings in late summer and root them in a moist, acidic medium.
*   **Seeds:** Requires 4–12 weeks of cold stratification; they are slow to germinate and grow.

## Benefits
*   **Ornamental:** Provides year-round green foliage, white summer flowers, and bright red winter berries.
*   **Wildlife:** Berries provide winter forage for birds.
*   **Edible/Medicinal:** Leaves are used for wintergreen-flavored tea. Contains natural analgesic compounds.

## Garden Placement
*   Ideal for woodland edges or as an understory plant for acidic-soil shrubs (like your blueberries or rhododendrons).
