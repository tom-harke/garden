= Pomegranate (Punica granatum)
== Overview
A hardy, ornamental, and fruiting shrub. Russian varieties like Salavatski have demonstrated exceptional cold tolerance.

== Varieties

=== Salavatski
 - *Characteristics:* Russian origin. High quality, large fruit with orange-red skin and red arils.
 - *Hardiness:* Zones 7-10. Reported to withstand temperatures as low as -6°F (in some cases survived 8°F in GA).
 - *Fertility:* Self-fertile.
 - *Notes:* Hard-seeded variety. Can be grown in colder zones (like 6) if given a protected site (e.g., south side of a house).

== Propagation
 - *Hardwood Cuttings:* The preferred method; take 6–10 inch cuttings of one-year-old wood in late winter and plant in well-draining soil.
 - *Seeds:* Germinate easily if cleaned of pulp, but will not be true-to-type; useful for ornamental purposes.
 - *Layering:* Ground layering or air layering are both highly successful during the growing season.

== Care Notes
 - *Sun:* Thrives in full sun.
 - *Soil:* Requires well-drained soil.
 - *Site:* Plant out of winter winds for extra protection, especially when young.
