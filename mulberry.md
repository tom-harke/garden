= Mulberry (Morus alba)
== Overview
Ornamental weeping (pendulous) form of white mulberry that produces sweet, black-colored fruit. Combines an edible harvest with a graceful, weeping habit suitable for smaller landscapes.

== Characteristics
 - *Tree:* Small, weeping habit. Mature size approx. 12 ft tall and wide.
 - *Fruit:* Small, black, very sweet.
 - *Zones:* 3-9.
 - *Fertility:* **Self-fertile.**

== Propagation
 - *Hardwood Cuttings:* Take 10–12 inch cuttings of dormant one-year-old wood in late winter; using a heat mat for bottom heat improves success.
 - *Layering:* Highly reliable for difficult varieties; pin a low branch to the ground or use air layering on a branch during the growing season.
 - *Seeds:* Easy but results in genetic variation and unknown sex; requires 60–90 days of cold stratification.

== Care Notes
 - *Training:* Young trees benefit from **staking and training** upward for the first few years to establish a desired mature tree height.
 - *Site:* Thrives in full sun. Prefers well-drained soil.
 - *Wildlife:* Note that fruit and foliage can attract deer.
 - *Maintenance:* Generally low-maintenance; no-spray required.
