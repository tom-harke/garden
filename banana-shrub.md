# Banana Shrub - *Magnolia figo*

Also known as **Michelia figo**, this evergreen shrub is a member of the Magnolia family, famous for its creamy-yellow flowers that emit a powerful fragrance of **ripe bananas** or cantaloupe.

## Growth Habit
*   **Size:** Slow-growing, typically reaching **6–10 feet tall** and wide.
*   **Foliage:** Dense, glossy, dark green evergreen leaves.
*   **Flowers:** Small, cup-shaped, creamy-yellow blooms with purple margins. Blooms primarily in late spring to early summer.

## Care in Zone 8b (Portland)
*   **Hardiness:** Reliably hardy in Zone 8b. However, it should be protected from strong, drying winter winds (like the East wind in Portland).
*   **Light:** **Partial shade** is ideal in the Pacific Northwest. Full sun can cause the foliage to become yellowish-green, while too much shade reduces flowering.
*   **Soil:** Prefers rich, slightly acidic, and well-drained soil. Amend heavy clay with compost to ensure winter drainage.
*   **Water:** Needs regular watering during the first few years and consistent moisture during Portland's dry summer months.
*   **Pruning:** Prune immediately after the spring bloom if shaping is needed, as it sets next year's flower buds by mid-summer.

## Edibility & Safety
*   **Caution:** Primarily grown as an **ornamental** shrub.
*   **Petals:** Some foraging sources report that the flower petals are edible with a clove/ginger flavor, but this is not widely documented.
*   **Fruit:** The small, reddish fruits are generally considered **non-edible**.
*   **Recommendation:** Treat as ornamental only unless you have confirmed a specific culinary use with a Magnolia expert.

## Propagation
*   **Semi-Hardwood Cuttings:** The most common method. Take 4–6 inch cuttings in late spring to early summer, dip in rooting hormone, and keep in high humidity until rooted (4–8 weeks).
*   **Air Layering:** High success rate for larger starts. Wound a pencil-thick branch in spring or summer, wrap in damp sphagnum moss and plastic, and wait 2–4 months for roots to develop.
*   **Ground Layering:** Very easy. Pin a flexible, low-hanging branch to the soil and cover with earth; it typically roots within 6–12 months.
*   **Seeds:** Possible but slow and erratic. Requires 6–8 weeks of cold stratification and may take 7–10 years to bloom.

## Garden Role
*   **Sensory Garden:** Best planted near a patio, window, or walkway where the banana fragrance can be enjoyed.
*   **Evergreen Screen:** Can be used as a dense, slow-growing privacy hedge.
