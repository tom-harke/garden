# Feverfew - *Tanacetum parthenium*

A versatile herb for pollinator support and medicinal use.

## Garden Benefits
*   **Pollinators:** Attracts bees, butterflies, and hoverflies.
*   **Natural Insecticide:** Contains pyrethrum-like compounds. Dried flowers can be steeped to make a pest-deterrent spray.
*   **Growth:** Hardy and drought-tolerant. Prolific self-seeder.

## Medicinal Uses (Consult professional before use)
*   **Migraines:** Historically used for prevention and treatment.
*   **Anti-inflammatory:** Used for arthritis and rheumatism.
*   **Cautions:** Can cause mouth ulcers if leaves are chewed fresh. **Do not use during pregnancy.**

## Ornamental
*   **Daisy-like flowers and aromatic, fern-like foliage.

## Propagation
* **Seeds:** Very easy; prolific self-seeder. Sow on soil surface in spring.
* **Cuttings:** Highly successful; 3-5" softwood in summer or basal in spring.
* **Division:** Divide mature clumps in spring or autumn.
