# PNW Seasonal Gardening Maintenance Schedule (Portland Metro, Zone 8b/9a)

## Winter (Dec - Feb)
- **Dormant Pruning:** Ideal time for most deciduous fruits (figs, bush cherries, grapes, caneberries) before buds swell.
- **Soil Management:** Perform soil tests for acid-loving plants while they are dormant. Amend with elemental sulfur if pH is > 5.5.
- **Winter Protection:** If freezing temperatures/snow are forecast, cover citrus and delicate passionflower. Ensure good drainage around lingonberries/blueberries to prevent waterlogging.
- **Dormant Spraying:** Consider horticultural oil/copper if you have a history of fungal/pest issues (e.g., rust on serviceberries).

## Spring (Mar - May)
- **Monitoring:** **CRITICAL** time for fungal issues due to spring rains (e.g., mildew on honeyberries, rust on serviceberries). Ensure plants have good airflow.
- **Mulching:** Apply pine needles or wood chips around acid-loving plants to suppress weeds, retain moisture, and keep roots cool.
- **Planting/Repotting:** Great time to establish new plants or repot container plants (kiwi, blueberries, figs).
- **Fertilizing:** Apply acidic fertilizers to blueberries/lingonberries once growth begins.

## Summer (Jun - Aug)
- **Irrigation:** Monitor moisture, especially for container plants (kiwi, honeyberry, arctic raspberry). Dry spells require consistent watering.
- **Training:** Actively train kiwi vines and grapes onto supports to prevent breakage.
- **Summer Pruning:** Light pruning for airflow if foliage is too dense, especially on stone fruits or caneberries.
- **Harvesting:** Continuous harvesting (raspberries, currants, gooseberries, juneberries, bush cherries).

## Fall (Sep - Nov)
- **Cleanup:** Remove and dispose of diseased plant material and fallen leaves (to prevent disease overwintering, especially under figs/serviceberries).
- **Winter Prep:** Final application of mulch to protect roots from potential winter frost.
- **Planting:** Excellent time to plant new perennials; the soil is still warm, and they can establish roots before spring.
- **Winter Protection Prep:** Ensure covers are accessible for citrus/passionflower.
