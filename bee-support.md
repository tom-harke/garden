# Supporting Carpenter Bees for Garden Pollination

Carpenter bees are essential "buzz pollinators" for complex flowers like passionfruit (Maypop). To support a healthy population that will aid your fruit harvest while protecting your property, follow these practices.

## 1. Provide "Controlled" Nesting Sites
Carpenter bees seek untreated, soft, weathered wood. Providing dedicated sites encourages them to nest away from your home's structural components.

- **Bee Blocks:** Drill holes in untreated softwood (pine or cedar) approximately 1/2 inch in diameter and 3–5 inches deep. Mount these in a sunny, dry, sheltered corner of the yard.
- **Natural Habitat:** Leave logs, brush piles, or dead standing trees in out-of-the-way areas of the yard if it is safe to do so.

## 2. Protect Your Home Structure
You can coexist with carpenter bees by making your home unattractive to them:
- **Paint or Stain:** Carpenter bees are attracted to bare, weathered wood. Painting or staining siding, window frames, and decks makes the wood unappealing to them.

## 3. Garden Practices for Bee Support
- **Pesticide-Free Environment:** Avoid all broad-spectrum insecticides, which are lethal to bees.
- **Floral Diversity:** Support them throughout the season with their favorite nectar sources:
    - Bee Balm (Monarda)
    - Goldenrod (Solidago)
    - Milkweed (Asclepias)
    - Salvia species

## 4. Understanding Behavior
- **Guardian Males:** In spring, males may hover aggressively. They lack stingers and are harmless; they are simply patrolling the nesting area.
- **Docile Females:** Females are the workers and are extremely docile, rarely stinging unless physically handled or trapped.
