# Grapes - *Vitis*

Vigorous perennial vines that are exceptionally well-suited to the Pacific Northwest. Both table and juice grapes can be grown successfully in Zone 8b.

## Varieties in the Garden

### **Niagara (*Vitis labrusca*)**
*   **Usage:** The standard white grape used for juice (e.g., Welch's). Versatile for fresh eating, jams, and jellies.
*   **Characteristics:** Very productive and extremely vigorous.
*   **Flavor:** Strong "foxy" aroma and sweet flavor typical of American labrusca grapes.
*   **Pollination:** Self-fertile.
*   **Care:** Fair disease resistance; usually requires at least one spray in the PNW to protect against black rot.

### **Suffolk Red**
*   **Usage:** A premier seedless table grape prized for its sweet, spicy flavor.
*   **Characteristics:** Produces medium-sized clusters of bright pink to ruby red berries. Thin-skinned with a melting, tender texture.
*   **Pollination:** Self-fertile.
*   **Ripening:** Mid-September in Zone 8b.

## Care in Zone 8b (Portland)
*   **Light:** **Full Sun** is mandatory (6–8+ hours) to ensure fruit ripens and develops sugar.
*   **Soil:** Deep, well-drained, and fertile loamy soil. Amend heavy clay to prevent winter root rot.
*   **Support:** Requires a **sturdy trellis**, arbor, or fence. Bines can grow 20+ feet if unmanaged.
*   **Pruning:** Heavy annual pruning is critical. Prune in late winter (February) to remove 90% of the previous year's growth.
*   **Water:** Consistent moisture during fruit development. Mature vines are moderately drought-tolerant but benefit from deep watering during dry spells.

## Propagation
*   **Hardwood Cuttings:** The most effective method. Take 12–18 inch dormant cuttings in late winter (February). Use pencil-thick wood with at least 3–4 buds.
*   **Layering:** Pin a low-growing vine to the ground in spring; it will root at the node over one growing season.

## Challenges
*   **Wildlife:** Birds love the ripening fruit; **netting** is often required as the grapes change color.
*   **Disease:** Ensure good airflow and prune for an open canopy to prevent powdery mildew and black rot.
*   **Safety Note:** Grapes are **highly toxic to dogs and cats**.
