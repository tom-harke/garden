= Honeyberry (Haskap)
== Overview
Hardy in Zones 3-7. Native to cold climates (Siberia, Russia). They are the first fruits to ripen, often earlier than strawberries.

**Zone Note for 8b/9a:** These plants perform best in colder zones (3-5). In warmer zones like 8b/9a, they may be shy producers or struggle with the heat. They prefer some shade in warmer climates.

Defaults
 - *Fertility:* **Not self-fertile.**

== Care Notes
*   **Sun:** **Full Sun.** Unlike in warmer regions, Honeyberries in the PNW thrive in full sun and require it for best fruit set.
*   **Water:** **High.** They have shallow roots and do not like to dry out. Provide 1–2 inches of water per week, especially during the spring fruiting season.
*   **Pollination:** Most varieties are not self-fertile. A 1:5 ratio of pollinators is recommended.
*   **Soil:** Adaptable, but has poor tolerance for wet soil (ensure good drainage).
*   **Pests:** Generally excellent resistance.

== Propagation
*   **Softwood Cuttings:** Take 4–6 inch cuttings in early summer (June/July) from non-fruiting stems for the highest success rate.
*   **Hardwood Cuttings:** Take 6–10 inch cuttings of one-year-old wood in late winter and stick directly into a nursery bed or pots.
*   **Layering:** Bend flexible branches to the ground in early spring and cover with soil; they will typically root by the following spring.
