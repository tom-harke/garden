| Variety | Fertility | Notes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Li** | Self-fertile | Large, sweet, crisp. Harvest in Sept. |
| **Lang** | Self-fertile | Bell-shaped. Harvest Oct. Sweet scent. |
| **So (Contorted)** | Self-fertile | Unique zig-zag growth habit. |
| **Shanxi Li** | Self-fertile | Largest Li type fruit (2"). Manageable tree. |
| **G-A 866** | Self-fertile | Extremely high sugar, date-like. Needs heat. |

### PNW Success Strategy
In Zone 8b, the primary challenge is a lack of summer heat.
- **Top PNW Varieties:** Honey Jar (earliest/sweetest), Sugar Cane, and Black Sea.
- **Heat Trapping:** Plant against south-facing walls to trap reflected heat.
- **Harvesting:** Pick at the "yellow-green" stage if the season is cool.

## Honeyberry Research Summary
Research into Honeyberries (Zone 3-7) based on Edible Landscaping (May 2026).

| Variety | Fertility | Notes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Blue Pacific** | Needs Pollinator | Large, sweet fruit. Russia origin. |
| **Berry Blue** | Needs Pollinator | Key pollinator for most varieties. |
| **Indigo Treat** | Needs Pollinator | High vigor (5.5ft), sweet/flavorful fruit. |
| **Beauty** | Needs Beast | Heart-shaped fruit. Best for Zone 8 with shade. |

**Caution:** Most Honeyberries perform best in colder zones (3-5). 'Beauty' is rated to Zone 8 but requires partial shade in hot summers.

### May 2026 Garden Additions
The following plants were added to the garden and documented:
- **Bearded Iris:** Special care for Zone 8b (1-inch planting depth, afternoon shade).
- **Banana Shrub:** Evergreen magnolia relative with banana-scented flowers (*Magnolia figo*).
- **Camellia sinensis:** The true tea plant; 2 plants added for a future "tea garden."
- **Goji Berry:** Nutrient-dense "superfood" shrub (*Lycium barbarum*).
- **Bunchberry:** Native PNW shade groundcover with edible red berries (*Cornus canadensis*).
- **Redwood Sorrel:** Native PNW shade groundcover with tangy, citrus-flavored leaves (*Oxalis oregana*).
- **Siberian Iris:** Moisture-loving companion for rain gardens.
- **Feverfew:** Dual-purpose pollinator support and medicinal herb.
- **Garden Loosestrife:** Upright perennial (was formerly Lysimachia).
- **Creeping Jenny:** Trailing groundcover/spiller.
- **Salvia 'Blackberry Ripple':** Long-blooming hummingbird favorite.
- **Leaf Celery:** Short-lived perennial for soups/stews (*Apium graveolens*).
- **Formosan Carpet Raspberry:** Tough, evergreen edible groundcover (*Rubus pentalobus*).
- **Caucasian Spinach:** Perennial climbing vegetable for shade.
- **Lemon Verbena:** Borderline hardy; must have excellent drainage and spring pruning.
- **Lemon Balm:** Bee powerhouse; requires deadheading to prevent spreading.
- **Wild Bergamot:** Mildew-resistant native; great for hummingbird moths.
- **Licorice:** Long-term root crop (3-4 years to harvest); nitrogen fixer.
- **Marshmallow:** Moisture lover for rain gardens; soothing medicinal roots.
- **Hardy Begonia:** Reliable shade perennial; re-emerges late in spring.
- **Corsican Mint:** Miniature groundcover with intense peppermint scent; currently in the Semicircle.
