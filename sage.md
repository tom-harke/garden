# Sage - *Salvia*

A diverse group of aromatic perennials, ranging from drought-tolerant shrubs to culinary herbs and hummingbird favorites.

## Varieties in the Garden
### **Culinary Sage (*Salvia officinalis*)**
*   **Characteristics:** Silvery-green, textured leaves with a strong, savory aroma.
*   **Use:** Essential for stuffing, meats, and savory dishes.
*   **Habit:** Evergreen sub-shrub; stays visible through PNW winters.

### **'Blackberry Ripple' (*Salvia microphylla*)**
*   **Characteristics:** Bicolor blooms with blackberry-purple centers and violet-pink edges. Scented foliage (blackcurrant/mint).
*   **Bloom Period:** Long season, from late spring until first frost.

### **Pineapple Sage (*Salvia elegans*)**
*   **Characteristics:** Intense pineapple-scented leaves and bright scarlet tubular flowers.
*   **Bloom Period:** **Late-season bloomer** (Sept–Oct). Vital for hummingbirds.
*   **Hardiness (Zone 8b):** Tender perennial. Protect crown with dry mulch in winter.

## General Care (Zone 8b/9a)
*   **Light:** Full sun is best for all varieties.
*   **Soil:** **Exceptional drainage is mandatory.** Highly susceptible to root rot in wet PNW winter soil. Use raised beds or add grit.
*   **Pruning:** Cut back in early spring only after new growth appears. Never cut into old, leafless wood.
*   **Wildlife:** Top-tier plants for hummingbirds and bees.

## Propagation
*   **Stem Cuttings:** Take 3–4 inch softwood cuttings in summer; they root easily in either water or a well-draining soil mix.
*   **Layering:** Pin a flexible branch to the ground in summer; it will root at the node and can be detached once established.
*   **Seeds:** Sow in spring; germination is generally good but slower than cuttings.
