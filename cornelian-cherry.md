# Cornelian Cherry - *Cornus mas*

A hardy, disease-resistant dogwood relative that produces edible tart-sweet fruit. It is an excellent choice for the Pacific Northwest (Zone 8b/9a) due to its resistance to dogwood anthracnose.

## Characteristics
*   **Bloom:** Very early (February/March). Bright yellow flowers provide critical early nectar for pollinators.
*   **Fruit:** Resembles an olive in shape but is bright red (or yellow). Flavor is like a cross between a cranberry and a plum.
*   **Hardiness:** Extremely hardy (Zones 4–8).
*   **Landscape Use:** Can be grown as a large shrub or a small multi-stemmed tree. 'Golden Glory' is a more upright cultivar suitable for smaller spaces.

## Recommended Varieties for PNW
For the best fruit quality, choose Ukrainian cultivars which have larger and sweeter fruit than the wild species.

| Variety | Fruit Type | Notes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Pioneer** | Large, pear-shaped | Very sweet and juicy; ripens late Aug/Sept. |
| **Elegant** | Dark red, pear-shaped | Heavy producer; excellent for fresh eating. |
| **Red Star** | Oval, dark red | Very high sugar content and complex flavor. |
| **Yellow** | Translucent yellow | Unique sweet-tart flavor; makes beautiful yellow preserves. |
| **Golden Glory**| Red (standard) | Best for small lots; upright habit and heavy bloomer. |

## Propagation
* **Layering:** Most reliable; pin low-growing branches to the ground in spring.
* **Cuttings:** Difficult; softwood in early summer or hardwood in late autumn.
* **Seeds:** Extremely slow; requires 2 years of stratification.

## Growing Conditions (Zone 8b/9a)
*   **Pollination:** Technically self-fertile, but **yields are much higher** with two different varieties for cross-pollination.
*   **Light:** Full sun to partial shade.
*   **Soil:** Adaptable to most soils (including clay), but prefers well-drained loamy soil.
*   **Water:** Drought tolerant once established, but requires consistent water in July/August for fruit development.
*   **Harvesting:** Fruit is very tart until "dead ripe." Wait until it falls from the tree or turns a very dark red and softens.
