# Leaf Celery - *Apium graveolens*

A rugged, biennial herb grown as a "cut-and-come-again" perennial in Zone 8b.

## Varieties for Zone 8b
*   **'Par-Cel':** Cold-hardy heirloom with parsley-like leaves and intense celery flavor.
*   **'Safir':** Resists bolting; excellent for leaf production.

## Care (Zone 8b/9a)
*   **Light:** Partial shade is mandatory during summer to prevent bitter leaves and bolting.
*   **Water:** **High moisture requirement.** As a marsh-native plant, it must never dry out. Mulch heavily.
*   **Soil:** Rich, loamy soil with high organic matter. Heavy feeder.

## Propagation
* **Seeds:** Most effective; sow in spring or late summer.
* **Division:** Possible by regrowing the base of a harvested plant in water or soil.
* **Cuttings:** Not recommended.

## Edibility & Uses
*   **Leaves/Stems:** Intensely flavored; best used in soups, stews, and stocks.
*   **Seeds:** Harvest in the second year for use as a spice.
*   **Safety:** Handle with care in bright sun (can cause skin irritation/phytophotodermatitis in some).
