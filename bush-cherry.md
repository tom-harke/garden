= Bush Cherry (Nanking Cherry)
== Overview
*Prunus tomentosa*. Hardy, compact shrubs that produce abundant small fruits. Can be used as foundation plants or fruitful hedges.

== Varieties


Default:
 - **Pollination:** Plant two different varieties for fruit set. White and red varieties can cross-pollinate.
 - *Fertility:* **Not self-fertile.** Planting two is recommended. Use another white variety (Ian, Jules) or a red Nanking for cross-pollination.


=== Gabe (White)
 - [Owned]
 - *Characteristics:* The **sweetest** of all Nanking cherries.
 - *Fruit:* Above-average size for Nanking type; white fruit.
 - *Tree:* 4-8 feet tall. Spacing: 6' circle.
 - *Zones:* 3-7 (Ian White variety is noted to handle 100°F+ heat in TX).

=== Ian (White)
 - to get
 - *Characteristics:* One of the sweetest Nanking varieties (along with Gabe). High heat tolerance (handles 100°F+).

=== Joel
 - [Owned]
 - *Characteristics:* A compact hybrid (*P. japonica* x *P. jacquemontii*) reaching 4-5 feet.
 - *Fruit:* Medium-sized, tart cherries.
 - *Ripening:* Standout late-ripening variety (late Aug/Sept).
 - *Fertility:* Partially self-fertile; yields better with 'Joy' or 'Jan'.
 - *Resilience:* Highly resistant to cherry worms, brown rot, and mildew.

=== Jules (White)
 - [Owned]
 - *Characteristics:* Part of the white Nanking collection.

=== Carmine Jewel
 - [Owned]
 - *Characteristics:* Dark red (almost black) fruit. Very sweet and nutrient-dense (superfood).
 - *Fertility:* **Self-fertile.**
 - *Growth:* 6-8 feet tall. Cross between *P. cerasus* (sour) and *P. fruticosa* (dwarf ground).
 - *Zones:* 2-7.
 - *Notes:* Extremely low-maintenance and pest-resistant.

=== Joy
 - to get
 - *Characteristics:* Fall-bearing bush cherry. 4' tall. Bloom/form like a flowering almond.
 - *Fruit:* Sour cherry-like; not bothered by birds.
 - *Fertility:* **Self-fertile.**
 - *Zones:* 3-8.

=== Red Varieties to consider
 - Denanka: Czech selection, hardy, productive. Partially self-fertile. Features traits of *P. besseyi*.
 - Bordowatta: Czech selection, deep red fruit, hardy, dependable.
 - Cheropugy

== Propagation
* **Cuttings:** Highly successful; softwood in early summer or semi-hardwood in late summer.
* **Division/Suckers:** Propagated by removing and replanting rooted suckers.
* **Seeds:** Requires 3-4 months of cold stratification; fruit quality may vary.

== Care Notes
 - *Sun:* Best in full sun.
 - *Soil:* Adaptable, but avoid wet soils.
 - *Maintenance:* Very low maintenance (no spray).
 - *Landscape:* Flowers overlap with Japanese plums.
 - *Pollination:* Generally not self-fertile; plant at least two different varieties for best fruit set.
