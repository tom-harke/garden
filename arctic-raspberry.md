## Propagation
*   **Division:** The most effective method. In early spring or late autumn, dig up a section of the colony and separate the horizontal rhizomes, ensuring each division has a healthy root and at least one bud.
*   **Root Cuttings:** Highly effective in late autumn or early spring. Cut pencil-thick rhizomes into 2–3 inch segments and lay them horizontally in moist, acidic soil.
*   **Seeds:** Requires 90–120 days of cold stratification. Surface-sow seeds as they need light to germinate; growth is much slower than vegetative methods.
*   **Cuttings:** Softwood cuttings can be taken in early summer and rooted in a humid, shaded environment.
