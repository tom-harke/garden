# Osoberry (Indian Plum) - *Oemleria cerasiformis*

The Osoberry is a native deciduous shrub of the Pacific Northwest and is one of the first plants to bloom in late winter (often February), making it a vital "harbinger of spring."

## Characteristics
*   **Growth Habit:** Fast-growing, multi-stemmed shrub or small tree. Reaches **8–15 feet tall** and 5–12 feet wide.
*   **Foliage:** Lance-shaped, lime-green leaves. They have a unique **cucumber scent** when crushed.
*   **Flowers:** Drooping clusters of small, greenish-white, bell-shaped flowers. Fragrance is a mix of almonds and watermelon.
*   **Fruit:** Small, olive-sized fruits that turn from yellow-orange to deep bluish-black when ripe in early summer.
*   **Dioecious:** Plants are either male or female. **Both are required** in close proximity for fruit production.

## Growing Conditions (Zone 8b/9a)
*   **Light:** Partial shade to full shade (natural forest understory). Can tolerate full sun if soil remains moist.
*   **Soil:** Prefers well-drained, loamy soil rich in organic matter. Tolerant of **clay** and sandy soils. pH 5.5–7.0.
*   **Water:** Prefers moist to dry-moist conditions. Established plants are drought-tolerant.
*   **Hardiness:** Hardy to **Zone 6** (down to 5°F).

## Propagation
*   **Seeds:** Best sown outdoors in autumn for natural winter stratification; otherwise, requires 3–4 months of cold, moist stratification.
*   **Suckers:** Dig up and transplant root suckers in early spring; ensure they have attached roots.
*   **Cuttings:** Take 6-inch softwood or semi-hardwood cuttings in late spring or dormant hardwood cuttings in late winter.

## Wildlife & Edibility
*   **Wildlife:** Critical early nectar source for **hummingbirds**, bees, and butterflies. Fruits are a favorite for birds and squirrels.
*   **Edibility:** Berries are edible but can be bitter or astringent. They are best when fully ripe (bluish-black). Consume in moderation (contains trace hydrogen cyanide).
*   **Landscape Use:** Excellent for woodland gardens, native hedgerows, and erosion control on banks.

## Maintenance
*   **Pruning:** Low maintenance. Prune in late winter to manage size or remove suckers to maintain a tree form.
*   **Pollination Note:** To ensure berries, plant at least one male and one female plant.
