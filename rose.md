# Roses - *Rosa*

A collection of classic ornamental shrubs, including heritage Tea roses and modern hybrids.

## Varieties in the Garden
*   **Tea Rose (Red):** Located in the backyard. Known for its spicy fragrance and bushy habit.
*   **Standard Roses:** Pink, Red, and Yellow varieties located in the courtyard.

## Care in Zone 8b/9a (PNW)
The primary challenge in our climate is fungal disease (Black Spot and Powdery Mildew).

*   **Sunlight:** **Mandatory morning sun.** Minimum 6-8 hours of direct sun. Morning sun dries the dew off leaves, which is the best defense against black spot.
*   **Airflow:** Space plants at least 3-4 feet apart. Do not crowd them with other plants. Good circulation is critical.
*   **Watering:** **Water at the base only.** Use a soaker hose or hand-water at the soil level. Never wet the foliage.
*   **Soil:** Rich, well-drained soil with plenty of organic matter.
*   **Mulching:** Apply 2-3 inches of compost or bark mulch in spring to keep roots cool and prevent soil-borne spores from splashing onto leaves.

## Maintenance
*   **Pruning:** 
    *   **Hybrid Teas:** Require heavy annual pruning in late winter (February/March) to encourage new flowering wood.
    *   **Old Garden Tea Roses:** Require very little pruning. Remove only dead, damaged, or crossing wood.
*   **Feeding:** Roses are heavy feeders. Apply a balanced rose fertilizer in early spring and again after the first bloom flush.

## Propagation
*   **Cuttings:** Take pencil-sized softwood cuttings in late spring or hardwood cuttings in late fall; use rooting hormone for best results.
*   **Layering:** Bend a flexible stem to the ground and cover a section with soil; it will develop roots over one growing season.
*   **Division:** Some bushy roses can be divided in late winter by separating the crown and root mass.
