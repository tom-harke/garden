# Hardy Begonia - *Begonia grandis*

A rare, reliably perennial begonia for the shade garden in Zone 8b.

## Care (Zone 8b/9a)
*   **Light:** **Full to partial shade.** No direct afternoon sun.
*   **Water:** Needs **consistent moisture**; do not let it dry out.
*   **Winter:** Root-hardy. Dies back to the ground and re-emerges **very late** (May).
*   **Propagation:** Produces tiny "bulbils" in leaf axils that drop and sprout new plants.

## Propagation
* **Bulblets:** Collect small bulbils from leaf axils in autumn and plant in spring.
* **Division:** Divide tubers in early spring before growth begins.
* **Seeds:** Fine as dust; sow on surface in spring.
* **Cuttings:** 4–6 inch sections taken in summer root easily in water or moist soil; individual leaves or leaf sections can also be rooted in moist media.

## Garden Uses
*   Late-season color (pink/white blooms from July-Oct).
*   Stunning olive-green leaves with red veining and burgundy undersides.
*   Excellent companion for ferns, hostas, and hellebores.
