# Lemon Verbena - *Aloysia citrodora*

A tender perennial shrub with the most intense citrus aroma of any herb.

## Care in Zone 8b/9a
*   **Hardiness:** Borderline hardy. It is **deciduous** and will drop all leaves below 40°F.
*   **Winter Warning:** It looks like "dead twigs" in winter and is often the last plant to wake up (May/June). **Do not dig it up** until summer if it appears dead.
*   **Soil:** Requires **exceptional drainage**. It will not survive wet winter roots.
*   **Sun:** Full sun (6-8+ hours) for maximum oil production.
*   **Pruning:** Cut back by 1/3 to 1/2 in spring once green growth appears to prevent legginess.

## Propagation
*   **Softwood Cuttings:** The most successful method; take 4–6 inch cuttings from green, flexible stems in late spring to mid-summer.
*   **Humidity:** Keep cuttings in a humid environment (like a plastic dome) as they dry out very quickly.
*   **Seeds:** Difficult and often nonviable; not recommended for home propagation.

## Uses
*   **Culinary:** Superior for teas, desserts, and seafood.
*   **Harvesting:** Highest oil content is in the morning.
