# Schisandra - *Schisandra chinensis* ('Eastern Prince')

Also known as **Magnolia Vine** or **Five-Flavor Berry**, this is a vigorous, deciduous vine valued for its medicinal berries. The **'Eastern Prince'** variety is particularly desirable because it is self-fertile.

## Care in Zone 8b/9a
*   **Hardiness:** Fully hardy. However, protect emerging spring growth from late-season frosts which can damage flower buds.
*   **Light:** **Partial shade.** Thrives as an understory plant or on a north-facing wall. Avoid intense afternoon sun.
*   **Soil:** Rich, fertile, and slightly acidic. Must stay **consistently moist**; not drought-tolerant.
*   **Support:** Twining vine that requires a sturdy trellis, arbor, or fence. Can reach 20-30 ft.

## Propagation
*   **Cuttings:** Take semi-hardwood or hardwood cuttings in summer (June–July); these are generally the most successful for cloning.
*   **Layering:** Long, flexible shoots can be layered in autumn; they will root over the winter and can be detached in spring.
*   **Seeds:** Best sown fresh in autumn or after 60–90 days of cold stratification in spring; germination can be slow and erratic.

## Pollination
*   **Self-Fertile:** Unlike wild-type *Schisandra chinensis* (which are typically dioecious), **'Eastern Prince'** is self-fertile and does not require a second plant for fruit production.

## Benefits
*   **Medicinal:** Berries are a powerful adaptogen in Traditional Chinese Medicine. Known as "Five-Flavor Berry" because they possess all five basic tastes (salty, sweet, sour, pungent, bitter).
*   **Ornamental:** Fragrant white flowers in spring followed by clusters of bright red berries in autumn.
