# Bunchberry - *Cornus canadensis*

Also known as **Dwarf Dogwood**, this is a low-growing, rhizomatous perennial native to the Pacific Northwest. It is highly valued for its striking dogwood-like "flowers" and clusters of bright red berries.

## Growth Habit
*   **Size:** Very low-growing, reaching only **4–8 inches tall**.
*   **Spread:** Spreads via underground rhizomes to form a dense, lush carpet.
*   **Foliage:** Whorls of glossy green leaves that often turn a stunning **burgundy or wine-red** in autumn. In the PNW, it can be semi-evergreen.

## Care in Zone 8b (Portland)
*   **Hardiness:** Fully hardy, but sensitive to high soil temperatures.
*   **Light:** **Partial to full shade.** It thrives in cool, dappled light and must be protected from intense afternoon sun.
*   **Soil:** Requires **acidic, moist, and organically rich soil**. It does best when mulched with pine needles or peat moss to keep the roots cool and acidic.
*   **Water:** Needs consistent moisture. It is **not drought-tolerant** and requires supplemental watering during Portland's dry summers.
*   **Maintenance:** Low maintenance once established, but it is delicate and **cannot handle foot traffic**.

## Edibility & Harvest
*   **Fruit:** Bright red berries (drupes) appear in late summer.
*   **Flavor:** Very mild and slightly sweet, with a mealy or gummy texture.
*   **Culinary Uses:** High in **natural pectin**, making them an excellent thickener for jams and jellies. Historically used by Indigenous coastal groups in puddings and sauces.
*   **Wildlife:** Berries are a favorite for songbirds.

## Propagation
*   **Rhizome Division:** The easiest method. In early spring or late summer, lift 4–6 inch sections of the horizontal rhizomes, ensuring each has a bud and fine roots.
*   **Seeds:** Slower and requires patience. Sow fresh seeds outdoors in fall for natural stratification, or provide 45 days of warm followed by 120 days of cold stratification.
*   **Stem Cuttings:** Take cuttings in July or August, ideally including a small piece of the rhizome, and root in a moist peat/sand mix.

## Garden Role
*   **Native Groundcover:** Perfect for woodland gardens, shady banks, or under the canopy of large conifers.
*   **Sensory Interest:** Multi-season interest with white bracts in spring, red berries in summer, and colorful foliage in fall.
