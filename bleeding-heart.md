# Bleeding Heart - *Lamprocapnos spectabilis* & *Dicentra formosa*

Woodland perennials prized for their delicate, heart-shaped flowers and elegant foliage. They are quintessential plants for the "Bedroom" and "Forest" zones.

## Varieties for the Garden
*   **Old-Fashioned Bleeding Heart (*Lamprocapnos spectabilis*):**
    *   **Appearance:** Tall (2–3 ft) with arching stems and large pink or white "hearts."
    *   **Best Zone:** **Bedroom.** Loves the gentle morning sun and protected environment.
*   **Pacific Bleeding Heart (*Dicentra formosa*):**
    *   **Appearance:** Lower growing (12–18 in) with lacy, fern-like foliage and smaller, clustered hearts.
    *   **Best Zone:** **Forest.** This PNW native is tougher, stays green longer into the summer, and thrives in deep shade.

## Care & Management
*   **Light:** Prefers partial shade. Morning sun with afternoon shade is the "Goldilocks" setting.
*   **Soil:** Rich, humusy, and well-draining soil. Amend heavy clay with compost or leaf mold.
*   **Water:** Keep soil consistently moist. If they dry out during a summer heatwave, they will enter dormancy early.

## The "Summer Vanish" (Dormancy)
Bleeding Hearts are **ephemeral**. By mid-July, the foliage will turn yellow and the plant will disappear entirely until next spring.
*   **Strategy:** Do **not** cut back foliage until it is completely yellow (it is storing energy for next year). 
*   **Companion Planting:** Plant near **Ferns, Hostas, or Hardy Begonias**. As the Bleeding Heart fades, these summer-vibrant plants will expand to cover the empty space.

## Safety Note
All parts are toxic if ingested. The foliage can cause skin irritation in some individuals; wear gloves when handling or pruning.
