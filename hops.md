# Hops - *Humulus lupulus*

Hops are vigorous, perennial climbing bines native to temperate regions. The Pacific Northwest is one of the premier hop-growing regions in the world, making them an excellent choice for a Portland garden.

## Growth Habit
*   **Size:** Extremely vigorous; can grow **15–25 feet** in a single season.
*   **Form:** Climbing "bines" (different from vines) that wrap clockwise around supports using stiff, hooked hairs.
*   **Foliage:** Large, deeply lobed green leaves.
*   **Structure:** They die back completely to the ground each winter and regrow from a perennial crown/rhizome in spring.

## Care in Zone 8b (Portland)
*   **Hardiness:** Fully hardy. They thrive in the Willamette Valley's climate.
*   **Light:** **Full Sun** is required for health and cone production.
*   **Support:** Requires a **very sturdy, tall trellis**, pole, or twine system (ideally 15+ feet).
*   **Soil:** Prefers rich, well-draining, sandy-loam soil. Amend Portland clay with plenty of compost.
*   **Water:** **High.** Hops are thirsty, especially in July and August. Use drip irrigation at the base to keep foliage dry and prevent mildew.
*   **Pruning:** Prune the first early shoots in April to encourage stronger, later growth and avoid early mildew. Train 2–4 bines per support and strip the bottom 2–3 feet of leaves once established for airflow.

## Propagation
*   **Rhizomes:** The most common method. Plant 2–6 inch root segments with at least two "eyes" in early spring (March–May).
*   **Cuttings:** Softwood cuttings taken in late spring root easily in moist media.
*   **Note:** Only female plants produce the cones used for brewing.

## Edibility & Harvest
*   **Hop Shoots:** The young, tender shoots pruned in early spring are a gourmet delicacy ("poor man's asparagus"). Sauté in butter or pickle.
*   **Cones:** Harvest in late August to mid-September when they feel dry, papery, and springy, and smell strongly "hoppy."
*   **Usage:** Cones are used for brewing beer, herbal teas, or medicinal sleep aids.

## Garden Role
*   **Privacy Screen:** Can create a dense green wall in just a few months.
*   **Vertical Interest:** Adds significant height and structure to the garden.
*   **Pollinator Support:** While wind-pollinated, the dense foliage provides habitat for beneficial insects.
