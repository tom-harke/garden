# Creeping Jenny - *Lysimachia nummularia*

A versatile, low-growing groundcover known for its bright chartreuse foliage and trailing habit.

## Care (Zone 8b/9a)
*   **Habit:** Mat-forming, only 2-4 inches tall. Vigorous spreader.
*   **Light:** Full sun to partial shade. Foliage color is most vibrant (golden/chartreuse) in sun; turns lime-green in shade.
*   **Soil:** Thrives in consistently moist, even boggy, soil. 
*   **Management:** Can be aggressive. Excellent as a "spiller" in containers or as a living mulch in moist, shaded areas.

## Propagation
* **Division:** Naturally roots at nodes; dig up and replant rooted sections.
* **Cuttings:** Highly successful; any stem section roots easily in water or moist soil.
* **Seeds:** Possible but rarely necessary due to vigorous spreading.

## Uses
*   **Landscape:** Groundcover between stepping stones, cascading over stone walls, or at the edges of ponds.
*   **Containers:** Popular as a trailing plant for pots to retain soil moisture.
*   **Edibility:** Low. Flowers and leaves can be used for a mild herbal tea.
