# Goji Berry - *Lycium barbarum*

Also known as **Wolfberry**, this deciduous shrub is a member of the nightshade family (*Solanaceae*) and is prized for its nutrient-dense berries and edible leaves.

## Growth Habit
*   **Size:** A vigorous, sprawling shrub that can reach **8–10 feet tall** and wide if unpruned.
*   **Form:** Arching, thorny branches. Often trained onto a trellis or pruned into a more compact bush shape.
*   **Foliage:** Narrow, gray-green leaves that are also edible.

## Care in Zone 8b (Portland)
*   **Hardiness:** Extremely hardy (to Zone 5). Well-suited for Portland's winters.
*   **Light:** **Full sun** is mandatory for heavy fruit production.
*   **Soil:** Prefers well-drained soil with a **neutral to slightly alkaline pH** (6.8–8.1). Portland soils tend to be acidic, so a light application of lime at planting is beneficial.
*   **Water:** Drought-tolerant once established, but needs consistent water during the first 2 years and during fruit set to ensure juicy berries.
*   **Pruning:** Fruits on **new wood**. Prune in late winter to remove dead wood and thin the center for airflow. Tip-pruning in early summer encourages lateral branches and more fruit.

## Propagation
*   **Cuttings:** Softwood cuttings taken in early summer root easily in moist potting mix.
*   **Suckers:** Dig up and transplant root suckers in early spring.
*   **Seeds:** Possible, but plants may vary in fruit quality from the parent.

## Edibility & Harvest
*   **Berries:** Harvest when fully red in late summer through frost. 
    *   *Note:* Avoid touching berries with bare hands if drying them, as skin oils can cause oxidation (turning them black). Use gloves or shake the branch over a tarp.
    *   *Flavor:* Sweet-tart; a cross between a tomato and a cranberry.
*   **Leaves:** Peppery and cress-like. Excellent in stir-fries or teas.
*   **Caution:** As a member of the nightshade family, some individuals with sensitivities may want to consume in moderation.

## Garden Role
*   **Edible Hedge:** Its thorny, arching habit makes it an effective (and fruitful) barrier.
*   **Container Gardening:** Because it can be aggressive (spreading via suckers), it is often grown in large 15+ gallon containers to control its size.
