# Caucasian Spinach - *Hablitzia tamnoides*

A rare, climbing perennial vegetable that thrives in the shade.

## Growth Habit
*   **Type:** Climbing vine (6-10 ft) using leaf petioles to cling to supports.
*   **Cycle:** Shoots emerge very early (Feb/March), flowering in summer, and dying back in winter.

## Care (Zone 8b/9a)
*   **Light:** **Partial to full shade.** The heat of Zone 8 summers will scorch leaves in the sun.
*   **Soil:** Rich, moisture-retentive, neutral-to-alkaline soil.
*   **Support:** Needs a sturdy, fine-meshed trellis or "wigwam" of poles.

## Harvesting
*   **Shoots:** Young shoots in early spring (asparagus-like).
*   **Leaves:** Heart-shaped leaves through spring/early summer. Flavor is mild, exactly like spinach.

## Propagation
* **Cuttings:** Highly successful; stem or basal cuttings in early spring.
* **Seeds:** Best sown in autumn for natural stratification; requires cold to germinate.
* **Division:** Large clumps can be divided in early spring as shoots emerge.
