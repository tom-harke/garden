# Corsican Mint - *Mentha requienii*

The smallest of the mints, forming a dense, moss-like carpet of tiny, fragrant leaves. It is famous for its intense peppermint aroma when stepped on or crushed.

## Characteristics
*   **Appearance:** Tiny, bright green leaves forming a flat mat only 1/4 to 1 inch tall.
*   **Flowers:** Tiny, inconspicuous lilac flowers in mid-to-late summer.
*   **Aroma:** Powerful peppermint scent, traditionally used to flavor Crème de Menthe.
*   **Hardiness:** Zones 6–9. In the PNW, it may die back in cold winters but often self-seeds or recovers from the roots.

## Care & Management
*   **Sun:** Prefers **partial shade** to dappled light. Can handle more sun in the PNW if kept consistently moist, but afternoon sun in the "Semicircle" can be intense.
*   **Soil:** Thrives in rich, moist, and well-draining soil. 
*   **Water:** **Crucial.** It is very sensitive to drying out. Must be kept consistently moist but not soggy.
*   **Foot Traffic:** Handles light foot traffic; ideal for planting between stepping stones.

## Containment & Spreading
*   **Growth Habit:** Creeping stems that root as they go. 
*   **Comparison:** While it spreads, it is far less aggressive and easier to remove than peppermint or spearmint due to its shallow roots and diminutive size.
*   **Ground vs. Pot:** In this garden, standard mints are restricted to pots. Corsican Mint is currently planted in the **Semicircle** ground as a fragrant groundcover, where it provides a "living mulch" effect similar to strawberries.

## Benefits
*   **Pest Deterrent:** Its strong scent can repel aphids and other pests.
*   **Culinary:** Edible leaves used for flavoring teas and desserts.
*   **Aesthetic:** Perfect for rock gardens, path gaps, or as a "spiller" in containers.
