# Candy Flower - *Claytonia sibirica*

A hardy, shade-loving native perennial (or short-lived perennial) that is a distinct relative of the common annual Miner's Lettuce (*C. perfoliata*).

## Characteristics
*   **Appearance:** Spatula-shaped leaves with beautiful pink or white striped flowers (giving it the name "Candy Flower").
*   **Life Cycle:** Unlike common Miner's Lettuce which is an annual, *C. sibirica* often behaves as a perennial in the moist, cool climate of the PNW.
*   **Flavor:** Has a distinct **earthy flavor**, often compared to raw beetroot or spinach. It is more robust in flavor than its annual cousin.

## Care (Zone 8b/9a)
*   **Light:** **Partial to full shade.** Perfect for woodland gardens, understory planting, or damp, shaded corners.
*   **Soil:** Thrives in rich, humusy, and consistently moist soil.
*   **Water:** Needs **consistent moisture**. It will go dormant or die back if allowed to dry out completely in summer.
*   **Habit:** Prolific self-seeder. It will naturalize easily in shaded areas to provide a lush, edible groundcover.

## Propagation
* **Seeds:** Most effective; prolific self-seeder. Sow in moist, shaded spots.
* **Division:** Divide established clumps in early spring.
* **Cuttings:** Not recommended.

## Edibility & Harvest
*   **Usage:** All aerial parts (leaves, stems, and flowers) are edible raw. 
*   **Nutrition:** Very high in Vitamin C.
*   **Season:** Can provide greens nearly year-round in Zone 8b if the location remains moist and sheltered.
