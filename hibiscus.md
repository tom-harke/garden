# Hibiscus - *Rose Mallow*

A diverse group of plants in the garden, ranging from woody shrubs to massive-flowered herbaceous perennials.

## Types in the Garden

### **1. Woody Hibiscus (*Hibiscus syriacus*)**
*   **Varieties:** 'Purple Pillar', 'Rose of Sharon'.
*   **Habit:** Deciduous woody shrubs that maintain their structure through winter.
*   **Flowers:** Smaller (2–4 inches), prolific blooms throughout summer.

### **2. Hardy Hibiscus (*Hibiscus moscheutos*)**
*   **Varieties:** 'Big Red' (likely *Lord Baltimore* or *Luna Red*) and 'Big White' (likely *Blue River II* or *Luna White*).
*   **Habit:** **Herbaceous perennial.** Dies back completely to the ground in winter.
*   **Flowers:** Massive **"Dinner Plate" blooms (6–12 inches across)**.
*   **Spring Emergence:** **Very Late.** Often does not sprout until late May or June. Do not dig up; it is likely just "sleeping."

## Care (Zone 8b/9a)
*   **Sun:** **Full Sun.** Both types require 6–8+ hours of direct sunlight for best blooming.
*   **Water:** 
    *   *Woody types:* Moderate; drought-tolerant once established.
    *   *Hardy (Swamp) types:* **High to Very High.** Must stay consistently moist. Do not let dry out during summer heat.
*   **Soil:** Prefers rich, organic-heavy soil. Hardy hibiscus can handle "wet feet" and heavy clay better than most plants.
*   **Pruning:** 
    *   *Woody types:* Prune in late winter to maintain shape.
    *   *Hardy types:* Cut dead stalks to 4–6 inches in late autumn after they turn brown.

## Propagation
*   **Woody (Rose of Sharon):** Take 5–6 inch softwood cuttings in late spring or early summer. named cultivars may not be true from seed.
*   **Hardy (Swamp Rose Mallow):** Best propagated by division in early spring as new growth emerges or by stem cuttings in early summer.
*   **Seeds:** Nick seed coats or soak in warm water for 24 hours before sowing to improve germination.
