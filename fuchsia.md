## Care (Zone 8b/9a)
*   **Sun:** **Partial Shade.** They love the PNW's cool, humid air. Morning sun with afternoon shade is ideal. 
*   **Water:** **Moderate to High.** Keep soil consistently moist but not waterlogged. They will wilt quickly in heat if the soil is dry.
*   **Maintenance:** **Do not prune in fall!** Wait until you see new green growth in late spring (April/May) to cut out dead wood; this protects the crown from winter rot.

## Propagation
* **Cuttings:** Highly successful; 3-5" softwood in spring/summer in water or soil.
* **Layering:** Trailing types root easily where they touch the ground.
* **Seeds:** Possible for species; sow in spring (hybrids do not come true).

## Recommended "Tasty" Varieties
While almost all fuchsia berries are edible, these are noted for the best flavor:
*   **_Fuchsia magellanica_:** The standard "Hardy Fuchsia." Spicy-sweet dark berries, very prolific.
*   **_Fuchsia procumbens_:** A trailing groundcover. Produces large, neon-pink, sweet, and juicy berries.
*   **'Jingle Bells':** Very hardy; known for larger, sweeter, darker berries.

### **Local (Portland Metro)**
*   **Portland Nursery:** The most reliable local source. Usually hosts a Hardy Fuchsia Show in August.
*   **Xera Plants:** Offers curated, climate-adapted varieties for the PNW.
*   **Cistus Design Nursery:** Excellent for *F. procumbens* and rare species.

### **Mail Order (PNW Specialists)**
*   **The Fuchsia Farm (fuchsiaweb.net):** Premier specialist with over 600 varieties.
*   **Dancing Oaks Nursery:** Great for both species and unique cultivars.
*   **Little Prince of Oregon:** Offers a solid "Hardy Fuchsia" collection for direct shipping.
*   **'Hawkshead' / 'Checkerboard':** Single-bloom types generally have better fruit than frilly "double" flowers.
