# Lingonberry - *Vaccinium vitis-idaea*

A low-growing evergreen shrub native to boreal regions, prized for its tart red berries and year-round ornamental value.

## Growth Habit
*   **Size:** Very compact, growing **6–12 inches tall**.
*   **Spread:** Spreads via underground rhizomes to form a dense mat. Individual plants typically spread 12–18 inches wide, eventually merging into a solid carpet.
*   **Garden Role:** Excellent as a "living mulch" or edible groundcover under taller acid-loving plants like Blueberries.

## Recovery Guide for Struggling Plants
If plants are showing yellowing leaves, wilting, or slow growth in Zone 8b:
1.  **Test Soil pH:** Must be between **4.5 and 5.2**. Add elemental sulfur or fast-acting acidifiers if above 5.5.
2.  **Improve Drainage:** Highly susceptible to root rot in heavy clay. Move to raised beds or mounds if soil is poorly drained.
3.  **Mulch Heavily:** Use 3–4 inches of acidic mulch (pine needles, sawdust, or peat moss) to protect shallow roots from heat.
4.  **Afternoon Shade:** Provide relief from intense afternoon sun to prevent leaf scorch.

## Care in Zone 8b/9a
*   **Light:** Full morning sun with filtered afternoon shade. Full sun is tolerable only if soil is kept consistently cool and moist.
*   **Soil:** Rich, sandy-loam, and **obligate acidic**. Mix in 50% peat moss when planting.
*   **Water:** Needs consistent moisture (1" per week). Do not allow to dry out completely.
*   **Fertilizer:** Use only acid-loving plant food. Avoid nitrate-based fertilizers.

## Pollination
*   **Requirement:** Technically self-fertile, but **planting 2+ varieties is highly recommended**.
*   **Benefits:** Cross-pollination between different varieties results in significantly higher yields and larger berries.
*   **Pollinators:** Specifically requires "buzz pollination" by bumblebees.

## Propagation
*   **Division:** Dig up and separate underground rhizomes in early spring before new growth starts.
*   **Stem Cuttings:** Take 2–4 inch semi-hardwood cuttings from June to September and root in a moist, acidic medium.
*   **Seeds:** Requires 2–3 months of cold stratification; surface-sow on acidic peat as they need light to germinate.

## Edibility & Harvest
*   **Flavor:** Very tart; best used in jams, jellies, and sauces.
*   **Harvest:** Usually produces two crops: a small spring crop and a larger fall crop.
*   **Wildlife:** Flowers attract bees; berries provide winter forage for birds.
