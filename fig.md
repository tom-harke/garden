= Fig (Ficus carica)
== Overview
Highly productive and adaptable fruit trees for the South.
Many varieties are well-suited for container growing and can thrive in Zones 7-9 (some up to Zone 6).

Unless noted otherwise, they are:
  - *Fertility:* Self-fertile.

=== LSU Purple
 - [Owned]
 - *Characteristics:* Highly reliable and prolific. Developed for adaptability in the South.
 - *Fruit:* Delicious, best harvested a few days after turning black, wrinkle, and **elongate**.
 - *Growth:* Ideal for containers; can bear fruit as soon as growth begins.
 - *Zones:* 7-9.

=== Brown Turkey
 - [Owned]
 - *Fruit:* Brown with amber flesh.
 - *Growth:* Thrives in large pots; good for "northern" regions if placed against a wall.
 - *Zones:* 6-9.

=== Hardy Chicago
 - *Characteristics:* Rich and sweet brown fig. Extremely reliable for outdoor culture.
 - *Growth:* Excellent for pots or against a protected wall.
 - *Zones:* 6-9.
 - *Notes:* Unique ability to regrow from roots if it suffers winter dieback and still produce fruit the same season.

=== Violet de Bordeaux (Negronne)
 - [Owned]
 - *Characteristics:* Small, purplish-black fig with rich strawberry-colored pulp.
 - *Reputation:* Widely regarded as one of the finest-tasting fig varieties.
 - *Growth:* Naturally dwarf/small tree.
 - *Zones:* 7b-9.
 - *Notes:* Closed eye (resists pests/spoilage). Ripens later than Hardy Chicago/Celeste.

== Propagation
* **Cuttings:** Highly successful; 8-12" dormant hardwood in late winter.
* **Layering:** Air layering or ground layering works exceptionally well in summer.
* **Seeds:** Not recommended; seedlings are often inferior.

== Care Notes
*   **Sun:** **Full Sun.** Figs require high heat to ripen fruit. Plant against south or west-facing walls to maximize heat units.
*   **Water:** **Moderate to High.** Provide 1–1.5 inches of water weekly during the growing season. While established trees are somewhat drought-tolerant, consistent moisture prevents fruit drop.
*   **Harvest:** Figs do not ripen off the tree. For best flavor, wait until they are fully colored, slightly wrinkled, and drooping.
*   **Winter Protection:** In Zone 8b, young trees benefit from wrapping in burlap or being placed against a protected south-facing wall during deep freezes.
*   **Pests:** Generally very good resistance, but watch for scale.
