# Redwood Sorrel - *Oxalis oregana*

A quintessential Pacific Northwest native groundcover, Redwood Sorrel is a lush, clover-like perennial that forms a beautiful carpet in shady garden areas.

## Growth Habit
*   **Size:** Low-growing, reaching **2–6 inches tall**.
*   **Spread:** Spreads via underground rhizomes. Can form extensive colonies in ideal conditions.
*   **Foliage:** Large, three-parted (trifoliate) heart-shaped leaves. A fascinating trait is **nyctinasty**: the leaves fold downward in direct sunlight or at night to protect themselves.
*   **Flowers:** Delicate white to pinkish five-petaled flowers bloom from early spring through summer.

## Care in Zone 8b (Portland)
*   **Hardiness:** Fully hardy. It is semi-evergreen in mild winters but may go dormant in extreme cold.
*   **Light:** **Partial to full shade.** It is highly sensitive to heat and direct sun.
*   **Soil:** Prefers humus-rich, acidic, and well-drained soil. It thrives in conditions that mimic the forest floor (leaf mold and compost).
*   **Water:** Needs **consistent moisture**. It is not drought-tolerant and will wilt during Portland's dry summers without supplemental irrigation.
*   **Maintenance:** Low maintenance. While it can spread, its shallow roots make it easy to control or transplant.

## Edibility & Harvest
*   **Flavor:** Leaves, stems, and flowers have a sharp, **tangy, lemony zing**.
*   **Usage:** Used fresh as a garnish, in salads, or to make a citrusy pesto.
*   **Caution (Oxalic Acid):** Contains oxalic acid (similar to spinach). Consume in **moderation**. Those with kidney stones or gout should avoid large quantities. Cooking the leaves reduces the acid content.
*   **Harvesting:** Can be harvested year-round in Portland. Pinch off individual stems to encourage new growth.

## Propagation
*   **Division:** Easiest method; separate the underground rhizomes in early spring or mid-September, ensuring each section has a growing point.
*   **Seeds:** Sow fresh seeds immediately as they have a short shelf life; they thrive in moist, shaded environments.

## Garden Role
*   **Native Groundcover:** Excellent for woodland gardens, replacing invasive ivies, or as a "filler" between ferns and wild ginger.
*   **Sensory Garden:** The folding leaves and bright flavor make it a great interactive plant.
