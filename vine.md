# Vines & Climbers

The garden utilizes various vining plants to maximize vertical space and provide structural interest. These range from heavy-fruiting woody bines to delicate ornamental and medicinal climbers.

## Primary Fruiting Vines
Detailed care and variety guides for the main fruiting vines can be found in their dedicated files:

*   **[Grapes](grape.md) (*Vitis*):** Vigorous producers of table and juice grapes. Includes Niagara and Suffolk Red.
*   **[Kiwis](kiwi.md) (*Actinidia*):** High-yielding vines including both Hardy (smooth-skinned) and Fuzzy varieties.
*   **[Passion Fruit](passion-fruit.md) (*Passiflora*):** Tropical and hardy varieties (Maypop) prized for their exotic flowers and tart fruit.
*   **[Hops](hops.md) (*Humulus lupulus*):** Extremely vigorous bines used for brewing and as a privacy screen.

## Other Vining Plants
*   **[Schisandra](schisandra.md) (*Magnolia Vine*):** A medicinal vine with "five-flavor" berries.
*   **[Caucasian Spinach](caucasian-spinach.md) (*Hablitzia*):** A perennial climbing vegetable for shade.
*   **[Clematis](clematis.md):** Primarily ornamental climbers (refer to inventory for specific varieties).

## General Care for Vines
*   **Support:** All vines require sturdy support (trellises, arbors, or wire systems). Match the strength of the support to the mature weight of the vine (Kiwis and Grapes require the heaviest support).
*   **Pruning:** Regular maintenance pruning is essential to manage vigor, prevent the "nesting" of pests, and encourage fruit production. Most fruiting vines are pruned heavily in late winter.
*   **Spacing:** Ensure adequate spacing (typically 8–15 feet depending on variety) to allow for light penetration and airflow, which are critical for preventing mildew in the PNW.
