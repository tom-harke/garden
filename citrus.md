= Citrus
== Zone 8b/9a

Can survive out doors
 - yuzu (C. ichangensis x C. reticulata, Zone 8-10) [Owned]
    - Exceptionally cold hardy (endures teens, potentially 0°F).
    - Loose skin, lemon-lime flavor, used for baking/seasoning.
 - some satsumas
    - Hardy Satsuma (trifoliate rootstock, Zone 8-10, self-fertile)
 - some blood oranges
 - Flying Dragon Trifoliate Orange (Citrus trifoliata var. monstrosa, Zone 6-9)
    - Hardiest variety (withstands below zero).
    - Ornamental corkscrew growth, seedy/resin-like fruit used for "citrus-aide" or tea.
    - Excellent for protective hedges (long curved thorns).

Cannot (Greenhouse/Indoor only):
 - lime
    - Thai Lime / Kaffir Lime (Citrus hystrix, Zone 9-10, aromatic leaves/rind)
 - lemon
    - Meyer Lemon
    - Spanish Pink Lemon (variegated)

## Propagation
* **Cuttings:** Highly successful; 4-6" semi-ripe in mid-summer with humidity and bottom heat.
* **Grafting:** Common for named varieties onto cold-hardy rootstock like trifoliate orange.
* **Seeds:** Primarily used for rootstock; polyembryonic seeds often produce clones.

## Care (Zone 8b/9a)
*   **Light:** **Full Sun (8-12 hours).** In the PNW, citrus needs maximum light and heat. Place against south-facing walls if possible.
*   **Water:** **Moderate.** Allow the top 2-3 inches of soil to dry out between waterings. Avoid "wet feet" during the rainy winter season.
*   **Growing Strategy:** Most varieties in this zone are best kept in **containers** to allow for winter movement into a garage or greenhouse when temperatures drop below 28°F.
