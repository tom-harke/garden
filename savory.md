# Savory - *Satureja*

A classic culinary herb known as the "Bean Herb" for its perfect pairing with legumes.

## Types of Savory
### **Winter Savory (*Satureja montana*)**
*   **Life Cycle:** Perennial (hardy to Zone 5).
*   **Habit:** Woody, semi-evergreen sub-shrub (up to 2 ft).
*   **Leaves:** Stiff, leathery, dark green.
*   **Flavor:** Strong, pungent, peppery, and piney. Best for long-cooking stews and heavy meats.

### **Summer Savory (*Satureja hortensis*)**
*   **Life Cycle:** Annual (must be replanted each year).
*   **Habit:** Delicate, bushy herbaceous plant (up to 1.5 ft).
*   **Leaves:** Soft, slender, light green.
*   **Flavor:** Sweeter and more refined than winter savory. The standard for *Herbes de Provence*.

## Care (Zone 8b/9a)
*   **Light:** Full sun.
*   **Soil:** Well-drained, sandy or loamy soil.
*   **Water:** Moderate moisture for summer savory; winter savory is quite drought-tolerant once established.

## Propagation
*   **Seeds:** Surface-sow in spring or fall; they require light to germinate and should not be covered with much soil.
*   **Cuttings:** Take 4–6 inch softwood cuttings in summer for both summer and winter savory.
*   **Division:** For winter savory, divide established woody clumps every few years in spring to maintain vigor.
