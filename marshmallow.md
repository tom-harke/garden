# Marshmallow - *Althaea officinalis*

A tall, beautiful perennial with soft pink flowers and "gooey" medicinal properties.

## Care & Growth
*   **Sun:** Full sun.
*   **Soil:** Loves **moist to wet soil**. Excellent for rain gardens or heavy clay spots.
*   **Size:** Reaches 4-6 ft tall; give it 2-3 ft of width.
*   **Maintenance:** Cut to the ground in late winter.

## Propagation
*   **Root Division:** Recommended method; divide mature root masses in early spring or late autumn, ensuring each piece has a growth bud.
*   **Seeds:** Require cold stratification for 4–12 weeks; sow in spring after scarifying the seed coat with sandpaper.
*   **Cuttings:** Softwood cuttings can be taken in early summer and kept in a humid, shaded environment until rooted.

## Edibility & Uses
*   **Roots:** The source of original marshmallows. High in mucilage; boil and fry as a vegetable or use in sweets.
*   **Leaves/Flowers:** All parts are edible. Young leaves in salads; flowers as garnish.
*   **Medicinal:** Powerful demulcent for sore throats, coughs, and acid reflux.
