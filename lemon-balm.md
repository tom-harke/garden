# Lemon Balm - *Melissa officinalis*

A vigorous, lemon-scented perennial from the mint family. A champion for bees.

## Care & Management
*   **Sun/Soil:** Extremely adaptable; thrives in full sun to partial shade.
*   **Containment:** Spreads primarily by **self-seeding**. To contain it, you must **deadhead flowers** immediately after they fade.
*   **Pruning:** Can be cut back to the ground multiple times a season to encourage fresh, aromatic growth.

## Propagation
*   **Division:** Very easy; divide established clumps in spring or autumn and replant immediately.
*   **Seeds:** Sow in spring on the soil surface; they require light to germinate.
*   **Cuttings:** Take 4–6 inch tip cuttings in late spring and root them in water or moist soil.

## Benefits
*   **Pollinators:** "Melissa" is Greek for honeybee; it is one of the best plants for attracting bees.
*   **Pest Deterrent:** The scent repels mosquitoes and cabbage worms. Good companion for brassicas.
*   **Medicinal:** Calming tea for stress and digestive support.
