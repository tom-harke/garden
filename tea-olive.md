# Tea Olive - *Osmanthus fragrans*

Also known as **Fragrant Olive** or **Sweet Olive**, this evergreen shrub is prized for its intoxicating, apricot-like fragrance that blooms in the fall and sporadically throughout the year.

## Varieties for Zone 8b/9a
*   **'Fudingzhu' (Nanjing's Beauty):** Highly prolific; produces massive clusters of cream-white flowers. Very fragrant.
*   **'Aurantiacus':** Features striking orange flowers with a heavy apricot scent. Blooms heavily in October/November.
*   **'San Jose' (*Osmanthus x fortunei*):** A hybrid variety that is **exceptionally hardy** for the PNW. Features holly-like leaves and powerful fragrance.

## Care (Zone 8b/9a)
*   **Light:** Full sun to partial shade. Full sun encourages the best blooming in the PNW.
*   **Soil:** Slightly acidic, well-drained soil. If you have heavy clay, plant on a slight mound.
*   **Water:** Needs consistent summer water for the first few years. Once established, it is moderately drought-tolerant.
*   **Placement:** Plant near walkways, entries, or windows to enjoy the "wafting" fragrance.
*   **Protection:** Protect young plants from cold, drying winter "East Winds."

## Propagation
*   **Cuttings:** Take semi-hardwood cuttings in late summer or early autumn; use rooting hormone and maintain high humidity for best results.
*   **Layering:** Air layering is effective for larger specimens during the active growing season.
*   **Seeds:** Rarely used as they are very difficult to germinate and extremely slow to grow.

## Maintenance
*   **Pruning:** Takes well to pruning. Can be maintained as a dense hedge or a small tree. Prune in late spring after the first bloom flush.
*   **Hardiness:** Generally hardy to 10°F. If a young plant loses leaves in winter, it will often "re-leaf" in spring.
