# Passion Fruit Collection

## Passiflora incarnata -- Maypop
- **Native:** North American.
- **Hardiness:** Zone 5–9 (Most cold-hardy).
- **Status:** Some consider it invasive due to aggressive spreading runners. Best in a contained area.
- **Uses:** Fruit is sweet-tart and edible (apricot-like flavor); flowers have medicinal properties. Leaves are also edible.
- **Pollination:** Self-incompatible. Requires cross-pollination from a genetically distinct *P. incarnata* seedling or a compatible species like *P. caerulea*. Pollinated by native bumblebees and carpenter bees.

## Passiflora edulis
- **Native:** South American.
- **Varieties:**
    - **Purple Possum:** Popular, relatively cold-hardy.
    - **Frederick purple:** Relatively cold-hardy.

## Care (Zone 8b/9a)
*   **Sun:** **Full Sun.** Needs a warm, sheltered spot to encourage prolific blooming and fruit set.
*   **Water:** **High.** These are vigorous vines that require consistent moisture to produce fruit. Water deeply once or twice a week during dry summer spells.
*   **Winter Care:** Mulch heavily in late autumn to protect the roots. Vines may die back in a hard freeze but will resprout in spring.

## Propagation
*   **Cuttings:** Take 6–10 inch semi-hardwood cuttings in spring or summer; root in a well-draining soil mix under high humidity.
*   **Seeds:** Use fresh seeds from ripe fruit; clean off the pulp and soak in water for 24 hours before sowing.
*   **Layering:** Very effective; pin a node to the ground and sever it from the parent once roots have formed.

## Recommended Pollination Partners
To ensure fruit production on your Maypop, these species are highly effective:
- **Blue Crown / White Crown (*Passiflora caerulea*):**
    - **Identity:** 'White Crown' is typically the 'Constance Elliott' cultivar.
    - **Compatibility:** Highly effective pollinator for Maypop.
    - **Aesthetic:** Blue Crown (classic blue/purple); White Crown (pure white).
    - **Safety:** Primarily ornamental; fruit is bland and contains cyanogenic glycosides.

## Strategy for Fruit Set
1. **Genetic Diversity:** Ensure Maypop vines are from different seed sources (clones will not pollinate each other).
2. **Cultivate Pollinators:** Carpenter Bees (*Xylocopa* spp.) are essential.
3. **Hand Pollination:** Use a brush to move pollen from anthers to stigmas during the one-day window the flower is open.
4. **Variety Addition:** Consider adding *P. caerulea* (Blue or White Crown) or a proven hybrid like 'Byron Beauty' to boost yields.
