# Juneberry - *Amelanchier*

Also known as **Serviceberry** or **Saskatoon**, these are beautiful shrubs or small trees that produce delicious, blueberry-like fruit in early summer.

## Varieties in the Garden
*   **'Jennybelle':** A vigorous variety (8–10' tall) known for large, exceptionally sweet, and juicy berries.
*   **'Regent':** A compact, mounded shrub (4–6' tall) that is very productive and ideal for smaller spaces.

## Care (Zone 8b/9a)
*   **Light:** **Full sun** is best for fruit production and vibrant red/orange fall color. Tolerates partial shade.
*   **Soil:** Prefers moist, well-drained, slightly acidic soil.
*   **Water:** Needs consistent moisture during summer for fruit development, though established plants are somewhat drought-tolerant.
*   **Pruning:** Minimal. Prune in late winter to maintain shape or remove suckers.

## Propagation
*   **Suckers & Division:** Dig up young suckers from the base of mature plants in late winter or early spring for the easiest results.
*   **Softwood Cuttings:** Take 4–6 inch cuttings in early summer (June) and keep in a high-humidity environment until rooted.
*   **Seeds:** Freshly harvested seeds can be sown immediately; stored seeds require complex stratification (warm followed by cold).

## Edibility & Harvest
*   **Flavor:** A unique mix of blueberry and sweet cherry with a subtle almond aftertaste from the soft seeds.
*   **Harvesting:** Ripens in June.
*   **Birds:** Birds will often strip the plant as soon as the berries turn red; use netting for a full harvest.
