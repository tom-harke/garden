# Galangal - *Alpinia*

A tropical perennial related to ginger, prized for its aromatic rhizomes used in Southeast Asian cuisine.

## Care (Zone 8b/9a)
*   **Hardiness:** Not hardy in Zone 8b. The plant will die if exposed to temperatures below 32°F, and rhizomes will rot in cold, wet winter soil.
*   **Winter Protection:** **Must be overwintered indoors.** 
    *   Move containers inside when night temperatures drop below 50°F.
    *   Alternatively, lift rhizomes in late fall and store in dry peat moss in a cool, dark, frost-free location.
*   **Sun:** **Full Sun.** Requires maximum heat and light in the PNW to develop rhizomes.
*   **Water:** **High.** Keep soil consistently moist but never waterlogged. Thrives in high humidity.
*   **Soil:** Rich, loamy, and well-draining. High organic matter is essential.

## Propagation
* **Division:** Best method; 2-3" rhizome segments with at least two "eyes" in spring.
* **Seeds:** Rarely used; requires high heat and humidity to germinate.
* **Cuttings:** Not recommended (stem cuttings).

## Harvesting
*   **Timing:** Rhizomes are typically ready to harvest 8-10 months after planting (usually late autumn in the PNW).
*   **Usage:** Greater Galangal (*A. galanga*) is the standard for Thai cooking; Lesser Galangal (*A. officinarum*) is stronger and more medicinal.
